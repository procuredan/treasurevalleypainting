Exterior House Painting in Idaho

Idaho's climate — hot, dry summers and cold winters with real temperature swings — is hard on exterior paint. A quality exterior job is built to handle that with the right prep, the right coatings, and the right timing.

The Prep That Makes It Last

  • Pressure or hand washing to remove dirt, chalk, and mildew
  • Scraping and sanding failed or peeling paint back to a sound edge
  • Caulking gaps and sealing around windows, doors, and trim
  • Priming bare wood and stained or repaired areas
  • Premium exterior coatings rated for UV and temperature swings

The Best Time to Paint in the Treasure Valley

Late spring through early fall is prime exterior painting season here — dry, mild stretches let coatings cure properly. Good crews watch overnight lows and midday heat and schedule around them, because paint applied in the wrong conditions won't bond or cure the way it should.

How Long a Good Exterior Job Lasts

With proper prep and quality paint, a professional exterior repaint commonly lasts many years before it needs refreshing — far longer than a rushed job over dirty or peeling surfaces. The prep is what buys you those extra years.
