Interior House Painting: What to Expect

A professional interior repaint is mostly about protection and prep. Before any color goes on the wall, a good crew masks floors and furniture, fills nail holes and cracks, sands rough spots, caulks gaps, and spot-primes repairs so the finish coat lays down smooth and even.

What's Included in a Quality Interior Job

  • Floors, furniture, and fixtures covered and protected
  • Walls patched, sanded, and caulked; repairs primed
  • Clean, straight cut-in lines at ceilings, trim, and corners
  • Two finish coats where needed for full, even coverage
  • Daily cleanup and a final walkthrough with you

Choosing Colors and Sheen

Flat and matte finishes hide wall imperfections and suit ceilings and low-traffic rooms; eggshell and satin are easier to wipe down for living areas and hallways; semi-gloss holds up on trim, doors, and in kitchens and baths. A good painter will help you match sheen to the room, not just the color.

How Long It Takes

Most interior projects are measured in days, not weeks — a few rooms often go quickly, while a whole-home repaint with heavy trim work takes longer. Your estimate will lay out the scope and a realistic schedule up front.
